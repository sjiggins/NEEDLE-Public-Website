Diffusion & Flow-Matching Models

Non-equilibrium inspired generative models to likelihood and posterior inference in high-energy physics

Overview

Develop non-equilibrium inpsired models, such as diffusion and flow-matching, for probabilistic inference in particle physics. These models learn to transform simple prior distributions into complex posterior distributions over physics parameters, enabling efficient sampling and likelihood evaluation for high-dimensional problems.

Model Architectures

Diffusion Models

  • Score-based models: Learning the score function of data distributions
  • DDPM variants: Denoising diffusion probabilistic models optimized for physics

Flow-Matching

  • Continuous normalizing flows: ODE-based generative models
  • Optimal transport: Flow matching with optimal transport objectives
  • Rectified flows: Straightening probability flows for faster sampling

Physics Applications

Posterior Inference

The diffusion/flow-matching based models enable tractable posterior/likelihood inference for complex physics scenarios where traditional methods scale poorly for high dimensional data. The resulting likelihood/posterior inference results achieve orders of magnitude statistical sensitivity to LHC particles physics problems.

Use Cases

  • Parameter estimation of Standard Model parameters in di-top quark production from proton-proton collisions at the LHC
  • Parameter estimation of Charge-Parity violating parameters in top quark production in association with a Higgs-boson, from proton-proton collisions at the LHC, as a portal into beyodn Standard model (BSM) physics.
